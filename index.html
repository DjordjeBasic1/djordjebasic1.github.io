About

I am a first-year clinical psychology PhD researcher jointly affiliated with Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam and the Technical University of Munich, supervised by Pim Cuijpers and Stefan Leucht.

My work asks two linked questions: how do we measure schizophrenia well, and what actually works for people with — or at risk of — psychosis. The first leads me into psychometrics and scale development; the second into large-scale meta-analysis and network meta-analysis of psychological and early interventions.

Much of this is collaborative and infrastructural: maintaining living evidence databases, coordinating an international expert consortium, and mentoring students who contribute to the underlying data.
